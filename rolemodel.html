Indira Gandhi was the first and only female Prime Minister of India. She served as Prime Minister from 1966 to 1977 and then again from 1980 until her assassination in 1984. Indira Gandhi was known for her strong leadership and her efforts to modernize India. She implemented various social and economic reforms, including the Green Revolution, which helped increase agricultural production in the country. Despite facing criticism for her authoritarian style of governance, Indira Gandhi remains a significant figure in Indian history and is often regarded as a role model for her determination and dedication to her country.
